\noindent We sincerely thank the reviewers for their constructive comments. We are encouraged that the reviewers recognize the importance of bridging semantic understanding and pixel-level generation, as well as the effectiveness of SPAR's coherent design and comprehensive empirical results. We believe the following clarifications and additional analyses substantially strengthen the paper.

\noindent[\textit{\Rone{XtYZ}, \Rtwo{F1M7}}.] \textbf{\Qtext{Q1: Novelty and contribution.}} \textbf{\Qtext{A:}} SPAR is best positioned as an applied/system-level contribution. Our claim is not that each primitive is entirely new, but that their coupling solves a concrete bottleneck in unified MLLMs: semantic encoders preserve understanding but lose pixel details. SPAR decouples semantic preservation and pixel reconstruction in the tokenizer, then uses this tokenizer as the generator's alignment target. We revise the paper to state this contribution more precisely.

\noindent[\textit{\Rthree{umgx}}.] \textbf{\Qtext{Q6: OpenUni comparison.}} \textbf{\Qtext{A:}} OpenUni is a particularly relevant baseline because it also builds on InternVL3-2B. SPAR-3B improves over OpenUni-L on GenEval (0.91 vs. 0.85) and WISE (0.64 vs. 0.52). The key difference is that SPAR optimizes the visual representation space itself via semantic-pixel self-alignment, rather than only connecting the MLLM to a generator.

\noindent[\textit{\Rtwo{F1M7}}.] \textbf{\Qtext{Q7: DTR vs. MoE.}} \textbf{\Qtext{A:}} DTR is not expert routing. MoE routes tokens to different parameterized experts and often needs dispatch/load-balancing losses. DTR routes each token over depths of the same MLLM hidden states, adds no expert networks, and is directly interpretable as token-wise layer preference.

\noindent[\textit{\Rtwo{F1M7}}.] \textbf{\Qtext{Q8: Self-alignment analysis.}} \textbf{\Qtext{A:}} We did not mean removing all pretrained visual backbones. The intended claim is narrower: SPAR removes the need for a separate external alignment teacher such as DINOv2/CLIP during generative training. The teacher is the same unified tokenizer used by SPAR, reducing understanding-generation space mismatch. We revise the wording and include convergence curves with/without self-alignment.

\noindent[\textit{\Rone{XtYZ}}.] \textbf{\Qtext{Q9: Presentation and citation issues.}} \textbf{\Qtext{A:}} We better explain Fig.~1(b), weaken/replace the blog-based citation [3] for the external-alignment claim, and fix the ``self-lignment'' typo.
